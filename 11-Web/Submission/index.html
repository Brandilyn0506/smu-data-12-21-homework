Summary Latitude vs X

For this project, we randomly selcted 500 countries using APIs and obtained data on each one's weather. We looked for relationships and correlation between latitude and temperature, latitude and humitiy, latitude and cloudiness, and latitude and wind speed. We broke the results off into Northen Hemisphere and Southern Hemisphere to discern the difference and variances of weather between cities closer to the equator an those further away. We ran linear regression to determine if we could accept our alternative hypothesis that cities closer to the equator have a more tropical climate. During our linear regression analysis we created scatter plots, plotted the line, created histograms and KDE's, created predicted vs actual graphs, and created an OLS regression summary.

From our analysis we were able to determine that as cities get closer to the equator, the temperature rises, and as cities move further away from the equator, the temperature decreases. We also determined that humidity and cloudiness have no correlation with latitude. We also determined that tropical areas tend to be most ideal locations to vacation due to ideal weather.
